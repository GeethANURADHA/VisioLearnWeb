Project Scope

Literature Survey

Prevalence of self-reported chronic eye conditions by age and sex, 2017–18

How Do Blind Students Write Their Exams? A Blind Girl Solving A Crucial Problem

The report highlights the challenges faced by visually impaired students in accessing IT education. Globally, 43 million people are blind, and 295 million have moderate to severe visual impairments. In Sri Lanka, 350,000 people are blind, and 340,000 have severe vision impairments.

Current e-learning platforms often rely on visual materials, disadvantaging visually impaired students. Voice-based chatbots, using speech recognition and synthesis, offer a promising solution by providing an accessible educational experience. These chatbots guide learners, answer questions, and assess knowledge through voice interactions.

However, many chatbots are not designed for visually impaired users. Traditional chatbots often use less sophisticated datasets like AIML, while more versatile options like JSON are better for inclusivity. While voice assistants like Alexa, Siri, and Cortana assist visually impaired users in daily tasks, their use in IT education is limited.

The lack of tailored support in IT education hinders the professional growth of visually impaired students. Voice-based chatbots can bridge this gap by offering personalized learning recommendations and knowledge assessments. Emotion detection technology, such as convolutional neural networks (CNNs), can enhance education by understanding students' emotional states, creating a supportive learning environment.

The study emphasizes the importance of inclusive education and the role of technology in providing equitable learning opportunities for all students.

Research Gap

Following are the research gaps identified in recent studies:

Voice-based Chatbot

Current chatbot systems lack personalized learning paths for visually impaired users.

Voice-guided Programming Lessons

Existing studies lack effective methods to teach complex IT concepts to visually impaired students.

Interactive Hand Gestures

Research is scarce on using gesture recognition technology to teach IT skills to visually impaired learners.

Emotion Detection

Integrating emotion detection into IT education for visually impaired students to enhance engagement and learning outcomes.

Research Problem

In what ways can online learning platforms facilitate connections between visually impaired students and potential employers in the IT industry, promoting inclusivity and equal opportunities for employment?

This study addresses significant educational and professional challenges faced by visually impaired students in IT. These students struggle with accessing digital content, impacting their learning and performance in IT courses, reducing their confidence, and limiting job market opportunities. Current educational methods relying on screen readers and tactile techniques often fail to provide comprehensive and engaging learning experiences, especially in complex IT subjects.

The project aims to develop and evaluate a voice-based chatbot integrated into e-learning platforms to create a more accessible and personalized learning environment for visually impaired students. This chatbot will enable students to interact with content via voice commands, enhancing independence and personalization. It will offer module recommendations based on user knowledge and interests, improving the educational experience.

The research also focuses on teaching programming to visually impaired students lacking prior IT knowledge. Starting with flowcharts, algorithms, and pseudocode to build computational thinking skills, the study will monitor progress through emotion and confidence detection. This data will help educators tailor the curriculum to meet students' needs better.

Additionally, the study explores innovative pedagogical approaches such as tangible flowcharts, algorithm usage, gamification, and activities to enhance understanding of computational thinking.

Proposed Solution

The primary goal of this research is to establish an accessible online learning environment that utilizes voice-activated technologies to enhance IT education for visually impaired students. The platform aims to eliminate current barriers to accessing digital content by offering easily navigable, engaging, and informative learning modules in critical IT domains such as security, business management, programming, and algorithms.

By integrating voice assistants, the platform will create a more inclusive and interactive learning environment, allowing visually impaired students to navigate and interact with instructional content through voice commands. Furthermore, the study will implement novel techniques for monitoring and assessing the development of visually impaired students, including metrics like mood and confidence detection. These insights will enable adjustments to instructional materials to better meet individual learner needs and preferences.

The research will also explore methods for connecting these students with potential employers in the IT industry. The platform will facilitate closing the skills gap between education and employment by enabling students to maintain profiles showcasing their achievements. This approach ensures that skills acquired through the e-learning platform translate into tangible job opportunities and fulfilling careers within the IT industry.

Research Objectives

Identifying Suitable Learning Modules through Chatbot

To create and deploy a voice-activated chatbot on the e-learning platform, helping visually impaired students select appropriate IT courses. The chatbot will utilize natural language processing (NLP) to interact with students, understanding their preferences, prior knowledge, and learning objectives to recommend tailored modules.

Implementing Speech Recognition for Quizzes

To develop a speech recognition system for quizzes that allows visually impaired students to orally respond. This system aims to make assessments more accessible and user-friendly, enabling students to participate in evaluations without traditional input devices like keyboards and mice.

Interactive Hand Gestures Integration

To integrate interactive hand gesture recognition technology into the e-learning platform, enabling visually impaired students to navigate and interact with educational content using simple hand movements. This feature aims to enhance user experience by providing an alternative interaction method complementing voice commands.

Development of Emotion Detection System

To implement an emotion detection system within the e-learning platform, enabling assessment of visually impaired students' emotional states. This technology will help tailor educational content to better meet individual emotional needs and enhance learning outcomes.
